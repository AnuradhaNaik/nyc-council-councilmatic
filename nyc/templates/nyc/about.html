About

New York City Councilmatic is a free tool for understanding and tracking legislation. It is the easiest way to access official New York City Council information.

Councilmatic helps you:

  • Stay up-to-date with the legislative activities of your New York City Council Member
  • See the schedule of public Council hearings and other meetings

What is New York City Council, and how does it work?

New York City Council is the lawmaking body of New York City. The Council comprises 51 council seats with each representative typically serving a four-year term. The Council is an equal partner with the Mayor in governing New York City. Its responsibilities include:

  • Discussing and approving local legislation
  • Making land use decisions
  • Approving the city's budget

Introductions are the most interesting type of legislation because they are proposals for new laws. Once an introduction is passed, it becomes a Local Law - see all Local Laws for 2015.

Most of the Council's legislative action happens in committees. Each committee meets in public hearings 2-3 times per month where proposed legislation is debated. Members of other government branches, as well as the public, are able to attend and comment at these hearings.

Meetings of the entire Council, referred to as Stated Meetings, occur twice a month at City Hall.

NYC Councilmatic is open source!

This website is open source and on GitHub - meaning that anyone can re-use or adapt the code.

Data

The data on this website comes from New York City Council's Legislative Research Center, a system built by Granicus using their Legistar Legislative Management Suite.

Daily, DataMade collects data from Legistar and the Legistar Web API.

NYC Councilmatic was originally developed by the non-profit Participatory Politics Foundation, with support from the Rita Allen Foundation. That version was launched for New York City in 2015 as a free, open-source, and open-data public resource for civic engagement.
